Hello Everyone!!

My name is Arupam Biswas, and I’m a Neuroscience Researcher exploring how the central nervous system heals, adapts, and thrives. My journey began in India and led me to Germany, where I am currently a PhD student at the Institute of Developmental Biology and Neurobiology (iDN), Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz. My research focuses on developing models and tools for next-generation therapies using precision-engineered artificial biomaterials to support CNS regeneration after traumatic brain injuries.

Outside of the lab, I love exploring new cuisines, traveling to discover different cultures, and sharing ideas with fellow scientists and enthusiasts. Collaboration and open-mindedness are key to my approach in science and life.

If you are interested in neurobiology, regenerative medicine, or want to collaborate, feel free to get in touch!

Research

PhD Topic: Interactive Biomaterials for Neural Regeneration (InteReg)

Traumatic CNS injuries and diseases like MS lead to lasting disability due to poor regenerative capacity. Barriers include glial scarring, inhibitory debris, lack of axonal guidance, and immune modulation. My work focuses on developing interactive biomaterials for neural tissue engineering, aiming to bridge injury gaps and promote regeneration using interdisciplinary approaches.

Master's Thesis: High-Content Screening for neurite growth analyses

This work involved developing automated high-content screening methods to evaluate the effect of genetic and pharmacological treatments on neurite outgrowth in cultured neuronal cells. The project contributed to understanding the cellular mechanisms underlying neuronal regeneration and identifying potential therapeutic targets.
