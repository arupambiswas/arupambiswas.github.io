Research

PhD Topic: Interactive Biomaterials for Neural Regeneration (InteReg)

The Interactive Biomaterials for Neural Regeneration (InteReg) project is a five-year collaboration between Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz, the Max Planck Institute for Polymer Research, and the Leibniz Institute for Resilience Research, aiming to develop precision biomaterials for treating neurological diseases. InteReg focuses on creating programmable materials that guide central nervous system cell regeneration following injuries or in conditions like multiple sclerosis and traumatic injuries (SCI, TBI).

Master's Thesis: High-Content Screening for neurite growth analyses: Evaluating gene treatments

Neurons in the central nervous system (CNS), including the brain and spinal cord, send out axons to make connections. In mammals, these axons can't regrow after injury, leading to permanent damage. Research shows that axon growth depends on certain genes called regeneration-associated genes (RAGs). Activating these genes can help axons regrow. In-vitro phenotypic screening is critical for evaluating the effects of these transcription factors on neuronal cells and identifying potential treatments to promote axon regeneration.
