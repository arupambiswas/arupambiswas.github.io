My name is Arupam Biswas, and I’m a Neuroscience Researcher exploring how the central nervous system heals, adapts, and thrives. Research

PhD Topic: Interactive Biomaterials for Neural Regeneration (InteReg)

Traumatic CNS injuries and diseases like MS lead to lasting disability due to poor regenerative capacity. Barriers include glial scarring, inhibitory debris, lack of axonal guidance, and more. The Interactive Biomaterials for Neural Regeneration (InteReg) project is a five-year collaboration between Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz, the Max Planck Institute for Polymer Research, and the Leibniz Institute for Resilience Research, aiming to engineer materials that can help guide and promote nerve regeneration.

Master's Thesis: High-Content Screening for neurite growth analyses: Evaluating gene treatments

Neurons in the central nervous system (CNS), including the brain and spinal cord, send out axons to make connections. In mammals, these axons can't regrow after injury, leading to permanent loss of function. My thesis investigated gene therapies that could stimulate regrowth, using high-content screening methods to analyze neurite extension.
