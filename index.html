Hello Everyone!!

My name is Arupam Biswas, and I’m a Neuroscience Researcher exploring how the central nervous system heals, adapts, and thrives. My journey began in India and led me to Germany, where I am currently at the Institute of Developmental Biology and Neurobiology (iDN). My research focuses on developing models and tools for next-generation therapies using precision-engineered artificial biomaterials to support CNS regeneration after traumatic brain injury.
Outside of the lab, I love exploring new cuisines, traveling to discover different cultures, and sharing ideas with fellow scientists and enthusiasts. Collaboration and open-mindedness are key to my approach.
If you are interested in neurobiology, regenerative medicine, or want to collaborate, feel free to get in touch!

Research

PhD Topic: Interactive Biomaterials for Neural Regeneration (InteReg)

Traumatic CNS injuries and diseases like MS lead to lasting disability due to poor regenerative capacity. Barriers include glial scarring, inhibitory debris, lack of axonal guidance, and more. Interactive Biomaterials for Neural Regeneration (InteReg) project is a five-year collaboration between Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz, the Max Planck Institute for Polymer Research, and the Leibniz Institute for Resilience Research.

Master's Thesis: High-Content Screening for neurite growth analyses: Evaluating gene treatments

Neurons in the central nervous system (CNS), including the brain and spinal cord, send out axons to make connections. In mammals, these axons can't regrow after injury, leading to permanent loss of function.
